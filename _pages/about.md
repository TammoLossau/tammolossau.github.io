I am a Visiting Assistant Professor of Philosophy at [Ashoka University](https://www.ashoka.edu.in/department/department-of-philosophy/). Before coming here, I received my Ph.D. at [Johns Hopkins](https://philosophy.jhu.edu) with a thesis on "Knowledge First Epistemology". Beginning in the summer of 2023, I will be a lecturer ("wissenschaftlicher Mitarbeiter") at the [University of Bremen](https://www.uni-bremen.de/philosophie) at the [chair of Norman Sieroka](https://www.uni-bremen.de/philosophie/forschung/theoretische-philosophie).

I am currently mainly interested in developing an epistemological framework around the idea that knowledge is a social kind. Roughly, the idea is that we develop a social standard of evaluating information that governs assertion. The development of this standard can, I think, be described using ideas from functionalist epistemology derived from Edward Craig. I have also done some work on the philosophical development of J.L. Mackie, and I am generally interested in the philosophy of religion.
